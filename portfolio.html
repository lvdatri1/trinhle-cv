AI Chatbot & Public Health

Kuini — AI Chatbot Helping Māori Women Quit Smoking

Led the architecture development of an AI-powered chatbot using Botpress to help Māori women quit smoking. The chatbot, named Kuini (Māori for "Queen"), was designed with culturally responsive conversational flows co-designed with Māori health practitioners, delivering personalised quit-smoking support through a natural-language interface. I architected the NLU pipeline, conversation orchestration, and integration with HPA's digital health ecosystem. The project demonstrated how conversational AI could be applied to indigenous public health — meeting users where they are with culturally appropriate digital tools. htrhn.org.nz/innovation/kuini

Botpress · NLP/ML · Cultural Responsiveness
Digital Inclusion & Public Health

Zero-Rated Data — Government Health Websites

Led the technical architecture of a zero-rated data joint project between the Ministry of Health, health agencies, and all New Zealand telcos (Spark, Vodafone, 2degrees). The initiative enabled New Zealanders to access government health websites without consuming mobile data, removing cost barriers to critical health information. I architected the framework for telco-side whitelisting, traffic classification, and reporting — a model designed to be scalable across other government services. The framework was subsequently adopted as a blueprint for broader government zero-rating initiatives. govt.nz/no-data-access

Ministry of Health · All NZ Telcos · Scalable Framework
